Understanding Dangerous Links

Scammers send fake emails and text messages that look real. These messages contain dangerous links that can steal your passwords, bank information, or personal details. Learning to spot them is important!

‚úÖ Examples of SAFE Links

https://www.amazon.com
https://www.google.com
https://www.facebook.com

These links are from real, trusted companies. They use "https" (the "s" means secure) and have simple, recognizable names.

üö® Examples of DANGEROUS Links

http://paypal-verify-account.suspicious-site.ru
https://www.faceb00k-security-check.com
http://192.168.1.1/amazon-login.html

These links look similar to real websites but have suspicious patterns. Scammers create these to steal your information.

‚ö†Ô∏è Warning Signs - What to Look For

  • Misspelled words: "faceb00k" instead of "facebook" (zero instead of letter O)
  • Strange endings: .ru, .tk, or unusual country codes instead of .com
  • Numbers instead of names: Like "192.168.1.1" - real companies use names, not numbers
  • Too many dots or dashes: paypal.verify.login.account.com has too many parts
  • Missing the padlock: URLs should start with "https" not "http" (no S)
  • Urgent messages: "Verify NOW!" or "Account will be closed!" creates panic
  • Too many dashes: verify-your-account-now-click-here.com looks suspicious
  • Wrong letters: amaz0n, g00gle, micr0soft use zeros instead of O's

üéØ How to Stay Safe Online

1. Never click links in unexpected emails or texts
If you didn't request it, don't click it. Scammers send millions of fake messages.

2. Type the website address yourself
Instead of clicking the link, open your browser and type the company's website yourself.

3. Look for the padlock icon
Real websites show a padlock icon next to the address. No padlock = not secure.

4. Use Silver Umbrella first
When in doubt, paste the link here and let us check it for you!

5. Trust your gut feeling
If something feels wrong or too urgent, it probably is a scam.

6. Call the company directly
If you get a message claiming to be from your bank, call them using the number on your bank card - NOT the number in the message.
